IoT helps businesses to simplify operations and increased labor costs. Smarter Natural Disaster Management

The ability to anticipate, with fine-grained precision, the advent of conditions encouraging forest fires before they get out of control or even start, allows containment teams to respond quicker, while allowing first responders to better coordinate targeted evacuations and to be more coordinated with the actual situation in the field. This definition can also refer to better prediction of flooding and/or enhanced reaction to mudslides, avalanches, earthquakes and other natural disasters.

Smarter Urban Management

Cities and counties automate traffic management that efficiently monitors and controls traffic movements on the basis of ever-changing conditions; car parking systems that intelligently direct cars to open spaces, minimize waste of time and resources and significantly reduce emissions; automate large-scale electricity use, production and delivery, all with a view to mitigation.

Smarter Healthcare

Wearable devices that detect a host of health problems, potentially even before they occur, and administer life-saving drugs immediately or deploy emergency responders with detailed information instantly placed in their hands or alert family members as needed. The ideas above barely skim the deep sea surface of opportunities offered by the coming IoT era, and here they all share a common (and not accidental) adjective: "smarter." If "smart" is characterized by the confluence of access to knowledge and the ability to use that knowledge in practical and effective ways, then the promise of the Internet of Things is simply a far "smarter" world that keeps us healthier, combines the personal good with the greater good, and increases the chances of the human race to provide future generations with a more prosperous legacy.
